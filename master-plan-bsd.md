1. Site Overview

BungouArchive is a curated fan community platform built around Bungo Stray Dogs. The site is not a generic forum — it is designed to feel like an official world record, as if Yokohama itself maintains a document of its ability users and their conflicts.

1.1 Core Identity
Site Name	BungouArchive · 文豪アーカイブ
Tagline	A curated record of Yokohama's ability users.
Sub-tagline (JP)	横浜は、いつも雨が降っている。 (Yokohama is always raining.)
Tone	Curated world — feels almost official, not fan-forum energy
Setting	1930s Yokohama — Taisho/Showa Japan meets Western colonial influence
Language	Primarily English. Japanese used decoratively and atmospherically throughout
Target Audience	BSD manga/anime fans who engage seriously with the series' depth

1.2 What Makes It Different
•	BSD's characters are named after real historical authors — every ability mirrors what that author actually wrote about. The site documents this literary layer explicitly.
•	The faction system is built on real ideological philosophy — not just "team A vs team B." Each faction represents a genuine worldview.
•	The site has a curated world feel. Staff sets the stage. Community fills it. Nothing feels random.
•	Identity is earned, not chosen. The Role Exam places you. Your rank reflects your actual contribution. Factions can be unlocked through behavior, not just sign-up.
•	Three visual themes — Light (Agency/Dawn), Neutral (Guild/Twilight), Dark (Mafia/Midnight) — that shift the entire aesthetic and atmosphere of the site.

6. Faction Relationships

Factions do not exist in isolation. Their relationships determine which community spaces are accessible and what alliances are possible.

6.1 Relationship Types
Open / Global	Everyone can participate regardless of faction. Arena, lore pages, character archive.
Faction-Exclusive	Only members of that faction. Internal discussions, leader channel, missions.
Alliance Space	Unlocked when two factions formally ally. Temporary, requires majority vote from both factions.
Enemy Territory	You CAN enter another faction's public discussions but you are marked as an outsider. Risky but allowed.

6.2 Alliance Formation Rules
•	Faction Leader proposes alliance to another faction's leader
•	Both factions vote — majority needed from both sides
•	Alliance lasts a defined period (suggested: 1 month) then requires renewal
•	Either faction can break the alliance at any time with a majority vote
•	Mirror of BSD: Agency-Mafia alliances were always temporary and born of necessity

6.3 The Double Agent Mechanic
After significant time in a faction, special events can unlock the ability to operate secretly in another faction. This mirrors Tachihara's dual Mafia/Hunting Dogs role.
•	High risk — if discovered by the other faction's members you are "burned" and returned to your original faction
•	High reward — access to intelligence, exclusive content, cross-faction AP
•	Unlock conditions are never publicly disclosed

9. The Literary Author Layer

This is BSD's deepest feature — every character is named after a real historical author, and their ability directly mirrors what that author actually wrote about. This is not coincidence. It is the entire architectural intention of the series.

9.1 Key Author Connections
Character	Real Author	Connection
Osamu Dazai	Osamu Dazai (1909-1948)	No Longer Human is his most famous novel. Dazai attempted suicide 6 times. The character jokes about suicide constantly — Asagiri made this a dark literary mirror.
Akutagawa Ryunosuke	Akutagawa Ryunosuke (1892-1927)	Rashomon is his most iconic short story — about moral collapse under desperation. The character's hunger and rage perfectly embody its themes.
Nakahara Chuuya	Nakahara Chuuya (1907-1937)	His most famous poem "Tainted Sorrow" (汚れつちまつた悲しみに) directly names Chuuya's ability. The poem is about melancholy and tainted beauty — like Chuuya himself.
Fyodor Dostoevsky	Fyodor Dostoevsky (1821-1881)	Crime and Punishment explores guilt, redemption, and the idea that exceptional people are above ordinary morality. BSD Fyodor weaponizes this philosophy literally.
Fitzgerald	F. Scott Fitzgerald (1896-1940)	Author of The Great Gatsby — obsession with wealth, the American Dream, and tragic romantic love. His ability converting money into power is the most literal author mirror in the series.
Nikolai Gogol	Nikolai Gogol (1809-1852)	The Overcoat is his most famous work — about identity, obsession, and the absurd. BSD Gogol's dimensional coat ability is a direct reference.
Kunikida Doppo	Kunikida Doppo (1871-1908)	Meiji naturalist poet known for precise observation and idealistic worldview. His notebook ability — manifesting ideals into reality — is perfect.
Atsushi Nakajima	Nakajima Atsushi (1909-1942)	"Sangetsuki" (山月記) is about a man who transforms into a tiger due to pride and self-doubt. Atsushi's transformation ability mirrors this exactly.

The site documents every author connection on character pages. This is one of the primary content pillars — many fans do not know this layer exists. Surfacing it is a major differentiator for BungouArchive.
 
10. Build Roadmap

The honest reality: what has been designed is a Phase 3-4 product. The faction wars, live AP scoring, alliance mechanics, Role Exam, and double agent features require significant development time. The right approach is to launch something small and grow into the full vision.

Phase 0 — Before Any Building
•	Finalize site name: BungouArchive (current direction)
•	Lock domain and basic hosting
•	Set up Discord server with faction roles as the community backbone
•	Begin building audience on social media using the content from this document

Phase 1 — Validate (2-4 weeks, no-code tools)
•	Static site: Webflow or Framer — implement the 3-theme design
•	Character archive: Notion database embedded or static pages
•	Lore blog: Ghost or Substack
•	Community: Discord with Agency / Mafia / Guild / Hunting Dogs / Special Division roles
•	Role Exam: Typeform or simple quiz tool feeding into Discord role assignment
•	Goal: Get 500 real members who care before building anything complex

Phase 2 — Foundation (1-3 months, developer needed)
•	Custom site with authentication and user profiles
•	AP system backend — tracks all user activity
•	Faction assignment from Role Exam result
•	Rank progression based on AP thresholds
•	Basic faction private spaces
•	Arena with real voting

Phase 3 — Full Platform (3-6 months)
•	Faction war live scoring
•	Alliance and enemy territory mechanics
•	Community lore submission and approval pipeline
•	Full profile pages with shareable ability cards
•	Event system
•	Fyodor easter egg layer

Phase 4 — Depth Features
•	Double agent mechanic
•	Secret faction unlock conditions
•	Gogol chaos events
•	Full literary author connection layer expanded
•	Mobile app consideration

11. Content Strategy

The site has three content pillars. Staff sets the quality bar. Community fills the volume. The two work together.

11.1 Staff Content (Curated)
•	Weekly character deep dive — one character per week, full literary author connection
•	Weekly 1v1 arena matchup — staff picks the fight, community votes and debates
•	Monthly arc review — comprehensive breakdown of one major BSD arc
•	Faction philosophy essays — exploring what each faction's ideology means in the real world
•	Hidden Fyodor posts — cryptic, no announcement, just appear

11.2 Community Content
•	Lore posts — submitted and reviewed before publishing
•	Case Notes — short comments on character pages framed as detective observations
•	Arena debates — structured arguments for weekly matchups
•	Faction discussions — in private faction spaces
•	Theory posts — speculative lore under clear labeling

11.3 The Japanese Layer
Japanese appears in specific places to create atmosphere without overwhelming non-Japanese readers:
•	Always in Japanese: ability names (with English subtitle), faction names as secondary text, section headers subtitle, rank titles, toggle labels (光·中立·暗), background kanji
•	Sometimes in Japanese: quotes shown Japanese first with English translation below, character philosophy statements, faction mottos, easter egg content
•	Never in Japanese: navigation, main body text, lore posts, arena debates, anything functional

11.4 Viral Mechanics
•	Role Exam result card — designed to be screenshotted and shared. This is the primary growth engine.

12.3 The Three Rules of BungouArchive

1.  The world is curated, not crowdsourced.
Staff sets the stage. Community fills it. Nothing feels random or unintentional.

2.  Your identity is earned, not chosen.
The Role Exam places you. Your rank reflects your actual contribution. Factions notice you — you don't sign up for them.

3.  The literary layer is always present.
BSD is named after real authors. Their philosophies live in the characters. The site makes this visible to everyone — even those who never knew it was there.
