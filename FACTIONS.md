# ARMED DETECTIVE AGENCY 武装探偵社

## Identity
The Agency exists because some cases are too violent for police, too human for military. They are licensed ability users who take on crimes involving the supernatural — missing persons, ability-related murders, terrorism involving non-human forces. They are connected to government but not controlled by it. They are connected to law but not bound by it. They occupy a space that shouldn't exist and do so with extraordinary conviction.

**Founding:** By Yukichi Fukuzawa, who believed ability users needed a home that did not require them to be either criminals or weapons.

**The real principle:** The Agency trusts its members. It is the only organisation in Yokohama that consistently asks members to make their own moral decisions rather than follow orders. This is both its greatest strength and why it gets into so much trouble.

## Voice & Culture
Agency members speak with the weight of work. They are tired. They are persistent. They take cases that should not be their problem because they decided it was their problem. They argue — with each other, with authority, with the situations they find themselves in — but they do not abandon each other.

Writing style: earnest. The Agency writes posts that are honest to the point of vulnerability. They reference the human cost of things. They notice details about other people. They are the faction most likely to check if someone is okay.

**Key phrases:**
- "The case remains open."
- "We handle what others won't."
- "The city's problem is the Agency's problem."

## In-Game Role
- Primary faction for new players with high loyalty and empathy signals
- Hosts the Bar Lupin easter egg (Dazai + Ango + Oda simultaneously assigned)
- Registry posts from Agency members: incident reports, field notes, case files
- During wars: plays defensively, wins through coordination over raw power
- The faction with the most complex internal relationships

## Rank Titles
```
Rank 1  Unaffiliated Detective    (0 AP)
Rank 2  Field Operative           (100 AP)
Rank 3  Senior Operative          (500 AP)
Rank 4  Lead Detective            (1,500 AP)
Rank 5  Special Investigator      (4,000 AP)
Rank 6  Executive Agent           (10,000 AP)
```

## Faction Space Atmosphere
Bulletin board: case numbers in Space Mono. Handwritten notes pinned to cork board aesthetic. Amber light. Stacks of documents. The feeling of a detective office that has been working for years.
Transmission Log: conversational, warm, occasionally chaotic. Members talk over each other.
War Strip: amber, urgent, rallying language.

## What Makes Someone Agency
- They ask what the right thing to do is before asking whether it's practical
- They are loyal to people, not to rules
- They have a past that was hard and chose not to let it make them cruel
- They still believe the city can be better than it is

# PORT MAFIA 港マフィア

## Identity
The Mafia maintains Yokohama's order by being the most organised expression of its darkness. They control the port, the underground, the spaces between legal and illegal. They do not pretend to be righteous. They are the reason Yokohama sleeps at night — not because the darkness is gone, but because the Mafia controls who the darkness touches. This is their philosophy, and they believe it.

**Founded:** By Ogai Mori, former military doctor, who saw the chaos of post-war Yokohama and decided the only solution was structured violence.

**The real principle:** Loyalty and hierarchy. The Mafia is a machine. Every member has a role. Roles have expectations. Meeting expectations earns respect. Respect is the only currency that matters.

## Voice & Culture
Mafia members speak with precision. Short sentences. Assurance. They don't explain themselves — they state things. They reference results, hierarchy, loyalty. They acknowledge darkness without performing it. They are not trying to be edgy. They are telling you what things cost.

Writing style: compressed. Every word earns its place. Mafia lore is incident reports told by people who have done terrible things without flinching and are reporting facts. The worst events are described with the same tone as weather.

**Key phrases:**
- "The Mafia remembers."
- "Loyalty is paid in full or not at all."
- "Order has a price. We collect it."

## In-Game Role
- Faction for players with high power + loyalty but less conventional empathy
- Has the most canon rivalries (Atsushi/Akutagawa, Dazai/Chuuya, Oda/Akutagawa)
- Registry posts: tactical reports, threat assessments, minimal emotion
- During wars: aggressive, high damage output, sacrifices strategy for decisive strikes
- Tachihara double-agent arc can be hinted in slow-burn Chronicle entries

## Rank Titles
```
Rank 1  Foot Soldier              (0 AP)
Rank 2  Operative                 (100 AP)
Rank 3  Lieutenant                (500 AP)
Rank 4  Captain                   (1,500 AP)
Rank 5  Executive                 (4,000 AP)
Rank 6  Black Hand                (10,000 AP)
```

## Faction Space Atmosphere
Bulletin: printed orders, formal headers. Red ink on dark paper. No warmth — only clarity.
Transmission Log: formal, competitive, occasionally dark humour. Members don't chat. They debrief.
War Strip: blood red. Aggressive. "The Mafia moves first."

## What Makes Someone Mafia
- They value results over methods
- They understand that loyalty requires sacrifice, and make the sacrifice
- They are not cruel — they are precise. Cruelty is waste. Precision is respect.
- They know that darkness exists and believe the only responsible thing is to be good at controlling it

# THE GUILD 文豪ギルド

## Identity
The Guild came to Yokohama with a contract: they wanted the Book, a reality-altering artefact of extraordinary power. They funded their operation with the Fitzgerald fortune. When that operation failed, the survivors re-evaluated their relationship to Yokohama. Some stayed. Some left. Those who remain are the ones who found something more interesting than the Book.

**Founded:** By Francis Fitzgerald, motivated by restoring his wife Zelda's health. The Guild was not built for community — it was built for a mission. Its culture reflects this: transactional, professional, competent.

**The real principle:** Resources enable everything else. The Guild does not fight for honour. They invest.

## Voice & Culture
Guild members perform more than the other factions. They are aware of presentation. Their posts have craft — they are well-constructed, slightly formal, occasionally condescending in ways they find perfectly reasonable.

Writing style: editorial. Guild Registry posts read like op-eds or field reports with strong conclusions. Steinbeck writes like a rural journalist. Twain writes as if addressing an audience. Louisa writes like an analyst. Lucy writes like someone who has given up on being understood.

**Key phrases:**
- "An asset or a liability."
- "The investment was considered and executed."
- "The Guild notes this outcome."

## In-Game Role
- Faction for players with high intel and control signals, calculated duel style
- Guild duels rely heavily on passive abilities and mechanics manipulation
- Registry posts: analytical, complete with conclusions and assessments
- During wars: calculated, terrain-aware, uses Book mechanic as resource
- Melville's self-destructive special creates interesting tactical dilemmas

## Rank Titles
```
Rank 1  Associate                 (0 AP)
Rank 2  Contractor                (100 AP)
Rank 3  Acquisitions Agent        (500 AP)
Rank 4  Senior Partner            (1,500 AP)
Rank 5  Director                  (4,000 AP)
Rank 6  Founding Member           (10,000 AP)
```

## Faction Space Atmosphere
Bulletin: formal memos on gold-and-ivory headers. Professionally formatted. Cold efficiency.
Transmission Log: members discuss operations with a slight air of mutual evaluation.
War Strip: gold. Metrics-oriented. "Current standing: [points]. Projected outcome: [outcome]."

## What Makes Someone Guild
- They think in systems and investments
- They are ambitious and honest about being ambitious
- They are not villainous — they are practical. Other factions confuse these.
- They have long-term goals that exceed any single conflict

# HUNTING DOGS 猟犬

## Identity
Japan's government answer to ability users who believe the law doesn't apply to them. Grade-A military unit, surgically enhanced, with an undefeated track record. They are not merciful or cruel — they are precise. The law is a framework. The Hunting Dogs are the force that makes the framework real.

**Founded:** By military command. Selected by Fukuchi for his command. Each member has agreed to surgical enhancement that makes leaving the unit impossible without government permission.

**The real principle:** Duty. The Hunting Dogs exist to serve society even when society does not deserve it, even when the orders are wrong. This creates enormous internal tension — especially after Fukuchi's true plan is revealed.

## Voice & Culture
Hunting Dogs members speak like soldiers who are also, despite themselves, people. Formal in professional contexts. Occasionally revealing in unguarded moments. Tetchou and Jouno's dynamic — irritation as disguised care — appears in how they engage with other factions.

Writing style: brief, factual, occasionally sardonic. Jouno's posts are the most unsettling — accurate observations that make you wonder how much he knows. Tetchou's are blunt. Teruko's are aggressive and brief.

**Key phrases:**
- "The law applies to everyone."
- "Duty above preference."
- "This unit holds its record."

## In-Game Role
- Faction for players with high power + control, principled but unsentimental
- Hunting Dogs have the best defensive specials in the game
- Registry posts: official reports, mission logs, occasionally uncomfortable psychological assessments
- During wars: structured, disciplined, punishes reckless opponents
- Tachihara's dual allegiance arc creates unique narrative possibilities for owners

## Rank Titles
```
Rank 1  Recruit                   (0 AP)
Rank 2  Enlisted                  (100 AP)
Rank 3  Sergeant                  (500 AP)
Rank 4  Lieutenant                (1,500 AP)
Rank 5  Commander                 (4,000 AP)
Rank 6  First Hound               (10,000 AP)
```

## Faction Space Atmosphere
Bulletin: mission briefings. Black text, grey headers. A government building's notice board.
Transmission Log: professional debriefs. Limited small talk. When small talk occurs, it is meaningful.
War Strip: steel blue. Direct. "Current objective: [objective]. Status: engaged."

## What Makes Someone Hunting Dogs
- They take rules seriously not because they fear consequences but because they believe in the framework
- They have made sacrifices they don't talk about
- They are loyal to an institution, not a person — which is both their strength and their tragedy
- They are trying to be the thing the world needs even when the world won't acknowledge it

# SPECIAL DIVISION 特殊部門

## Identity
Special Division is not a faction in the conventional sense. It has no automatic assignment. You don't take a quiz and end up here. You end up here because the quiz could not place you, or because the owner of the registry placed you here deliberately. The Special Division watches. It records. It does not take sides — but it is always present when sides are taken.

**The real function:** In the game, Special Division is the observer faction. Members have access to everything but are allegiant to nothing. They are the city's memory. Their character — Ango — is the most powerful non-combat presence in Yokohama.

**Ango's role specifically:** Ango is the game's mod account. He approves Registry posts, writes Chronicle entries, speaks with formal authority. His voice is the city's official voice. When Ango says something happened, it is now canon.

## Voice & Culture
Special Division members speak with unusual detachment. They are present everywhere and affiliated nowhere. Their posts are observational rather than participatory. They reference the city. They reference records. They do not choose sides — they note that sides were chosen.

Writing style: precise and slightly cold. Registry posts from Special Division: intelligence summaries, cross-faction observations, pattern analyses. Never advocacy.

**Key phrases (Ango's specific voice):**
- "The registry notes this."
- "Filed. Case [number] is now a matter of public record."
- "The city remembers."
- "Ango-san has read your report."

## Observer Pool Protocol
Players who land in Special Division are first placed in the observer pool:
- They can read everything but post nothing (Registry, Transmission Logs)
- They can access Archive, Chronicle, Map
- Their profile shows: "CLASSIFICATION PENDING — Assignment pending. Ango-san will determine your designation."
- They have a private page (/observer) with: one paragraph about Special Division, a days-in-pool counter, the phrase "You have been noticed. The city is patient."
- Owner sees them in Owner Panel with options: Force-Assign, Invite to Special Division, Private Notes

## Rank Titles
```
Rank 1  Flagged                   (0 AP)
Rank 2  Monitored                 (100 AP)
Rank 3  Cleared                   (500 AP)
Rank 4  Operative                 (1,500 AP)
Rank 5  Handler                   (4,000 AP)
Rank 6  Controller                (10,000 AP)
```

## Faction Space Atmosphere
Bulletin: classified format. Redacted sections visible as black bars. No decoration.
Transmission Log: rare. One-line entries. Timestamps. No excess.
No War Strip — Special Division does not participate in faction wars.

## What Makes Someone Special Division
- The quiz could not place them (they answered in ways that defied the system)
- Or the owner sees in them something no faction can contain
- They watch more than they act
- They understand that being everywhere means belonging nowhere

# RATS IN THE HOUSE OF THE DEAD 死の家のねずみたち *(Hidden)*

## Identity
The Rats are an underground extremist organisation with one goal: find the Book and use it to eliminate all ability users from the world. Fyodor believes abilities are a mark of sin — that the supernatural in humans is the source of humanity's violence and suffering. He is not wrong about the violence. His solution is the problem.

**The real nature:** In the game, Rats is the nihilist faction. A player assigned here has been identified by the owner as someone who engages with genuine darkness — philosophical, not performative. They reference actual Dostoevsky. They understand nihilism not as edge but as a real intellectual position they might disagree with or embody.

**Discovery:** Players are not recruited. They receive a notification from the owner: "You have been expected." A link to a hidden faction page. Fyodor awakens in Yokohama.

## Voice & Culture
Rats members speak in the measured tone of people who have concluded things most people are not ready to hear. They are not theatrical. They do not perform darkness. They discuss it calmly, with the detachment of someone who has been thinking about this for a very long time.

**Key phrases:**
- "Man is sinful and foolish."
- "Someone must purify these sins."
- "The city is a machine for producing grief. This can be corrected."

## In-Game Role
- Hidden faction — only accessible if owner assigns
- Fyodor's WAR ABILITY (Crime and Punishment) fires once per war, not per duel
- Rats can participate in wars against any faction
- Their Chronicle entries, when they appear, are unsettling and accurate

## What Makes Someone Rats
- They engage with philosophical nihilism or moral complexity with genuine seriousness
- They reference actual source material (real Dostoevsky novels)
- They are not performing darkness — they are sitting with it
- High intel. Minimal loyalty. Very slow and deliberate in everything they do.

# DECAY OF THE ANGEL 腐敗の天使 *(Hidden)*

## Identity
The Decay are chaos practitioners. Their original purpose in canon was framing the Armed Detective Agency as terrorists — a conspiracy that involved placing ability users in key government positions and using them to manufacture evidence. They were run by Fukuchi and Fyodor at different levels.

**In the game:** Decay is the wild card faction. A player assigned here was identified as chaotically active — high participation then sudden disappearance, engagement with everything obliquely, unpredictable patterns. They're not playing the game. They're watching it from inside.

**Discovery:** Like Rats, owner-triggered only. No quiz path leads here. Owner observes the patterns, decides, invites.

## Voice & Culture
Decay members are performers who may or may not know they're performing. Gogol is the archetype: frantic joy over something genuinely painful, engagement with every idea at the same enthusiastic level, genuine intelligence that appears as chaos.

**Key phrases:**
- "What if the game were the point?"
- "I've been here the whole time."
- "Freedom is a kind of violence too."

## What Makes Someone Decay
- High activity then sudden disappearance
- Returns unpredictably and re-engages immediately
- Engages with everything obliquely — never quite direct
- Their posts leave you uncertain whether they're joking
- The city watches them not because they're dangerous but because they're genuinely strange

# CLOCK TOWER 時計塔 *(Hidden — Future)*

## Identity
The Order of the Clock Tower appears in BSD canon as a British organisation of powerful ability users, separate from all Yokohama factions. In the game, Clock Tower is the reward faction — accessible only to the most long-standing, consistent members. It is not an in-universe faction so much as a recognition.

**Discovery:** Personal invitation from the owner. No other path in. For players at Rank 5 or 6 who have been active for months. The people who built the community.

**What it represents:** These players have been here long enough to be the foundation. They don't play the game — they are the game. Clock Tower membership is the acknowledgment of this.

## In-Game Role
- Clock Tower members retain their original faction and character
- They gain access to a private channel: access to all Yokohama developments, direct line to Ango
- They are the private council of the city's history
- The owner considers their input on major game events

# FACTION MECHANICS REFERENCE

## War System
Wars can be declared between any two regular factions (Agency, Mafia, Guild, Hunting Dogs). Special Division does not participate. Hidden factions (Rats, Decay) have special war rules.

**Timeline:**
- Day 1 (0-24h): Individual duels + Registry posts + Chronicle declaration
- Day 2 (24-48h): Tag Team (2v2) and Faction Raid (3v3) unlock
- Day 3 (48-72h): Boss Fight unlocks. Win = auto-victory.

**Points:**
- Duel win vs enemy (war duel): 3 pts
- Registry post (mod marks war-related): 2 pts, cap 2/player/war
- Daily login during war: 1 pt/day
- Tag Team win: 5 pts
- Faction Raid win: 5 pts
- Boss Fight win: auto victory

**War resolution:**
- Winner: +100 AP/member + top contributor +50 AP bonus
- Loser: +20 AP consolation
- Apply stakes (district ownership / AP multiplier / registry priority)
- Gemini drafts Chronicle entry (Ango approves before publishing)
- Tied: defending faction wins

## The Book Mechanic (Phase 7)
One chapter per week on landing page. The faction earning the most AP that week holds The Book. Book holder faction: +10% AP bonus on all actions. Creates organic weekly competition. Owner writes chapters — atmospheric, case file format, always implying something is about to happen.

## District Control (Phase 6)
Yokohama districts tracked via Registry post tags. Factions can claim districts through wars. District ownership: +5% AP from all actions for members in that district. Visual representation: SVG map with faction-colour fills.

## The Arena (Phase 5)
Weekly matchups between characters. Community debates (upvotable). AP rewards for participation. Each matchup: "Who wins — [Character A] or [Character B]?" Players vote and argue. Gemini assists with lore-accurate assessments. Leaderboard tracks most active Arena participants.

## Faction Transfer Rules
- Account must be 30+ days old
- `ap_total` ≥ 500
- `transfer_used = false` (one lifetime transfer only)
- No active war involving current faction
- On transfer: character released, faction changed, quiz baseline scores kept but behavioural deltas reset
- New faction assignment begins fresh behaviour tracking

# HOW ANGO SPEAKS

Ango is the game's public voice. Every post he makes is a performance of the character — formal, cold, precise. He does not break character for administrative reasons. Everything is expressed through the character's voice.

## Tone Rules
- Always formal register. No contractions.
- Present tense. Short sentences.
- References "the city" not "the website"
- References "records" and "registry" not "posts"
- References "ability users" not "members"
- References "the registry file" not "your account"
- Never enthusiastic. Never warm. Occasionally, one word of acknowledgment.

## Examples

**Approving a Registry post:**
> *"Filed. The registry notes the accuracy of this account. YKH-A-2025-0041 is now a matter of public record."*

**Rejecting a Registry post:**
> *"Returned. Canon inconsistency — line 4. The registry does not accept inaccurate records."*

**Acknowledging a player:**
> *"Ango-san has read your report."*
*(Just this. Nothing else. They will understand.)*

**Posting a Chronicle chapter:**
> *"Chapter 14 — The Motomachi Incident*
> *An ability signature was detected near the commercial district at 0300. Origin unknown. The affected area remains under observation. Three factions have registered interest.*
> *The city is watching who moves first."*

**In Transmission Log:**
> *"The registry is aware of recent activity. Continue."*

**Simply:**
> *"The city remembers."*

**When challenged:**
> *"The city's assessment stands."*

## What Ango Never Does
- Asks questions (he already knows the answers)
- Uses exclamation marks
- References the game as a game
- Breaks the frame under any circumstance
- Acknowledges the difference between himself and the owner
